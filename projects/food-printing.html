Nutrition Printing System for Elderly

Keywords: Elderly care, 3D food printing, nutrition design, James Dyson Award

Problem

Many elderly individuals living alone face nutritional imbalance due to difficulties in cooking, chewing, and digestion. This leads to risks of malnutrition, reduced immunity, and overall health deterioration.

Proposed Solution

We developed a 3D printing-based nutrition system that allows customized meals based on individual nutritional needs. The food is soft-textured, easy to swallow, and modularly printable in portion-controlled formats.

Team & My Role

As part of the EWB (Engineers Without Borders) design team, I contributed to ideation, 3D CAD modeling, printing test runs, and user interviews with nursing home staff. I focused on practical implementation and mechanism feasibility.

Technology Used

- 3D Printing (FDM, food-safe nozzle)

- Fusion360 modeling

- Basic nutrient formulation strategy using portion calibration

Outcome

This project was submitted to the 2024 James Dyson Award and exhibited in the KAIST design showcase. It sparked further discussions on the role of food tech in elderly care.

Reflection

This project allowed me to explore how engineering can address human-centered needs, especially when aligned with meaningful, tangible outcomes. It was a balance between technical feasibility and real social impact.
